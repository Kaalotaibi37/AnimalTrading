Seahorse

Seahorses prefer to swim in pairs with their tails linked together. They swim upright and avoid predators by mimicking the colour of underwater plants.

  • Scientific Name: Hippocampus
  • Average Length: 35.56 centimeters
  • Average Lifespan: 2 years
  • Habitat: coral reefs, seagrass beds, and mangrove forests

Seahorses are fascinating examples of bony fish. They have a unique body morphology with a horse-shaped head, large eyes, curved trunk, and a prehensile tail.
